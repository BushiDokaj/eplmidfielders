The Premier League's Greatest Midfielders


In 2017 the BBC Sport YouTube channel released "Match of the Day's Premier League all-time XI", a video in which Alan Shearer, Ian Wright, Danny Murphy and Gary Lineker attempt to name the best ever Premier League starting 11. In the video, when tasked with choosing two central midfielders for their squad, the footballers considered for the honour are:

  • Roy Keane
  • Patrick Vieira
  • Frank Lampard
  • Stephen Gerrard
  • N'Golo Kanté
  • Xabi Alonso
  • Paul Scholes
  • Yaya Touré
  • Bryan Robson

Unfortunately, because this happened in 2017, some incredeble current players were left out of this list, including Kevin De Bruyne and David Silva. Still, the BBC pundits, all former legends of the game, were able to use their expertise to assess the players and pick who they believe are the best two. As an engineering student, without the the same football experience, I thought it would be interesting to attempt to use statistics as a means for tackling this problem.

Prior to 2006, the Premier League collected very little data. For this reason, Patrick Vieira, Roy Keane, and Bryan Robson, all of whom had either retired or left the league by then, could not be included in this analysis for lack of data. While Bryan Robson was a brilliant footballer, he only played a small part at the end of his career in the Premier League. Keane and Vieira, however, both played massive roles in the success of their teams. Keane was the captain of a United team that that dominated the league for years. Vieira was the captain of an incredible Arsenal team who managed to go invincible. Paul Scholes will also be harmed by the lack of data prior to 2006 as most of his best years were before then. Fortunately, Scholes played well into his 30's, giving us enough data to include him.



FRANK LAMPARD

Frank Lampard was one of the most prolific attacking midfielders in the Premier League era. His sheer offensive output rivals that of elite strikers. He will always be remembered as one of the greatest midfielders England has ever produced.

Player Profile
STEPHEN GERRARD

Gerrard was the complete midfielder. A player who could score, defend, spread the ball and dictate the play. The Liverpool skipper will forever be remembered at Anfield as a truly world class footballer. His love for, and dedication to the city of Liverpool has cemented his role as a club legend.

Player Profile
N'GOLO KANTÉ

N'Golo Kanté is the only player on this list who still plays in the Premier League today. A crucial piece in the Leicester team that won the title in 2015, he then went on to win a second Premier League at Chelsea. Kanté is undoubtedly one of the best defensive midfielders today.

Player Profile
XABI ALONSO

Standing alongside Gerrard in a Liverpool side that made two Champion's League Finals and won one, was Xabi Alonso. Depending on the system, Xabi could play as a 6 or an 8 and do both at the highest level. He was a world class midfielder, deserving of his spot on this list.

Player Profile
PAUL SCHOLES

Paul Scholes was the midfield maestro at Manchester United. He completed passes that only a handful of players could do. Scholes was a key piece of the United dynasty that dominated the Premier League from its inception up until his departure along with Alex Ferguson in 2013.

Player Profile
YAYA TOURÉ

Yaya Touré was an elite midfielder who played for the top clubs in Europe, including Manchester City. The Ivorian won three league titles with City. Touré is another example of the complete midfielder. He was a 6 with the ability to drive forward and score goals as well.
